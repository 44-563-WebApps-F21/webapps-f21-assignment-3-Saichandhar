Circle-Line Intersection

To determine the position of a line with respect to a circle, we’ll find its distance from the center of the circle. Let d be this distance and r be the radius of the circle. Then, if d < r , then the line cuts the circle at two distinct points. if d = r, then the line touches the circle at one point. if d > r, then the line does not intersect/touch the circle.
